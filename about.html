About me

The story behind 20 years of water & sanitation science, policy, and sustainability leadership.

My story

Science that
serves people.

I started my career in Mumbai, studying microbiology with a curiosity about what lives in the water we can't see — and what happens when it reaches us. That question has driven every chapter of my work since.

My PhD at the National University of Singapore took me deep into the detection and inactivation of waterborne pathogens in tropical freshwater systems. What surprised me most was not the complexity of the science — it was how far removed the science was from the people who needed it most.

That gap became my professional focus. At TÜV SÜD under the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation's sanitation programme, I helped shape ISO 30500 and ISO 31800 — the first international standards for non-sewered sanitation technologies — working with government delegations and testing labs across more than 15 countries. For the first time, I could see how a methodology developed in a laboratory could directly improve sanitation access for millions.

At Ashoka University, I had the chance to build something from scratch: India's first sustainability office at a liberal arts university. Founding that office — from policy to operations to India's first university menstrual waste recycling programme — taught me that institutional change is also a form of science: evidence-based, iterative, and fundamentally about people.

Today I am completing the Women Independent Director Leadership Programme at IIM Ahmedabad, and actively seeking advisory board roles where science, sustainability, and governance intersect.
