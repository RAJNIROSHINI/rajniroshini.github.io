Publications & research

Peer-reviewed journals, book chapters, conference presentations, and a US patent.

Water Safety · 2014 · PLOS ONE

Microbial Quality and Phylogenetic Diversity of Fresh Rainwater and Tropical Freshwater Reservoir

What microbial communities live in Singapore's tropical reservoirs and how does rainwater affect their safety? This study mapped the full diversity of microbes — including pathogens — in a tropical freshwater system, contributing to evidence for reservoir water safety monitoring.

Kaushik R, Balasubramanian R, Dunstan H (2014). PLOS ONE, 9: e100737.

Circular Economy · 2014 · Bioresource Technology

Enzyme-assisted hydrothermal treatment of food waste for co-production of hydrochar and bio-oil

Can food waste be converted into useful fuels and materials? This research developed an enzyme-assisted process to extract bio-oil, biochar, and chemicals from food waste — a circular economy solution for waste management in urban settings.

Kaushik R, Parshetti GK, Liu Z, Balasubramanian R (2014). Bioresource Technology, 168.

Water Safety · 2013 · Water Research

Inactivation of Escherichia coli Using Gene Silencing and Chlorination: A Comparative Toxicity Assessment

Chlorination is the world's most common water disinfection method — but can gene silencing match or improve on it? This paper compared both approaches for killing E. coli, opening new directions in pathogen inactivation research.

Kaushik R, Balasubramanian R (2013). Water Research, 48: 519–528.

Air Quality · Water Safety · 2013 · Science of the Total Environment

Discrimination of viable from non-viable Gram-negative bacterial pathogens in airborne particles using PMA-assisted qPCR

Standard tests often detect dead bacteria alongside live ones, overstating risk. This paper developed a method to distinguish live from dead airborne pathogens — making air quality monitoring far more accurate for public health purposes.

Kaushik R, Balasubramanian R (2013). Science of the Total Environment, 449: 237–243.

Air Quality · Water Safety · 2012 · Applied and Environmental Microbiology

Influence of Air Quality on the Composition of Microbial Pathogens in Fresh Rainwater

When polluted air meets rain, what happens to the water? This study showed how air quality directly shapes the microbial content of rainwater — with implications for drinking water safety in dense urban environments.

Kaushik R, Balasubramanian R (2012). Applied and Environmental Microbiology, 78(8): 2813.

Air Quality · Water Safety · 2012 · Atmospheric Environment

Assessment of Bacterial Pathogens in Fresh Rainwater and Airborne Particulate Matter using Real-Time PCR

Using real-time PCR, this study assessed how bacterial pathogens move between air and water — building the evidence base for integrated air-water quality monitoring in tropical cities.

Kaushik R, Balasubramanian R (2012). Atmospheric Environment, 46: 131–139.

Water Safety · 2011 · Critical Reviews in Environmental Science and Technology

Methods and Approaches for Detection of Cyanotoxins in Environmental Samples: A Review

Cyanotoxins from algal blooms are a growing threat to drinking water globally. This comprehensive review mapped existing detection methods and gaps — becoming a reference for researchers and water safety regulators worldwide.

Kaushik R, Balasubramanian R (2011). Critical Reviews in Environmental Science and Technology.

Water Safety · 2009 · Sensors and Actuators B: Chemical

Real-time PCR-based microfluidic array chip for simultaneous detection of multiple waterborne pathogens

Detecting multiple pathogens in tap water simultaneously, in a handheld device? This paper described a breakthrough microfluidic platform that makes rapid, portable water pathogen testing possible — a tool directly relevant for low-resource settings.

Ramalingam N, Rui Z, Liu HB, Dai CC, Kaushik R, et al. (2009). Sensors and Actuators B: Chemical.

Book Chapter · Nanotechnology

Biological Synthesis of Silver Nanoparticles and Assessment of their Bactericidal Activity

Exploring green synthesis routes for silver nanoparticles — materials with potential for water treatment and antimicrobial applications — and testing their effectiveness against bacteria.

In: Green Nanotechnology and the Environment. Elsevier, pp. 107–120.

Book Chapter · Water Safety

Biological Contaminants in Drinking Water: Emerging Issues and Regulatory Trends

An overview of emerging biological threats to drinking water safety and how regulatory frameworks are (and aren't) keeping pace — aimed at policymakers and water safety professionals.
